A-Arm Design

September 2022 - January 2023

Project Overview

Developed Skills
  1. Redesigned a new set of A-arms for our solar car
  2. Consulted research papers to find information of suspension design using matrices
  3. Created a MATLAB script to take input forces and hardpoint locations from excel, then plot and compute suspension forces
  4. Created CAD in Solidworks for upper and lower control arms based on simulation result
  • SolidWorks Design
  • Suspension Design
  • MATLAB
  • Simulation Creation

Objective

This project was assigned to me by my team lead in the vehicle dynamics subteam of UBC Solar. I was tasked with designing the A-arms for our new vehicle Brightside.


A-Arms used by Formula UBC

Initial Research

To start by defining the problem, other car team suspension designs were investigated.

We also consulted UBC Formula to see what they did for their A-arms - they used staked bearings into aluminum and carbon fiber tubes.


Concept Generation

The next step was to start concept generation for potential solutions for the A-arm.

Force Calculation

The next step was to calculate the forces on the A-arms under the maximum loading condition (max brake, bump, and steer), so that we can determine how strong they need to be.

The initial UCA FBD following the method previously used on the team can be seen in the figure on the left.


Matrix Force Solving

The FBD method that was previously used on the team did not accurately represent the A-arms, so another approach was needed.

Looking at research papers on the calculation of forces in control arms, a method using matrices to represent any 6 member system was found.

FBD for 6 member suspension


3D MATLAB suspension plot

MATLAB Script

To implement the calculations, MATALB was used to read any arbitrary input force and hardpoint locations from an excel spreadsheet for the A-arms to determine the forces experienced in the members.

The hard points and the forces are also plotted in 3D space. An output plot can be seen on the left.


A-arm hardpoint and input force spreadsheet

A-Arm CAD

Using the forces calculated on the A-arms, the detailed design was done, choosing off-the-shelf components and creating CAD in Solidworks.

The factor of safety on the LCA front, which experiences the most force during the loading condition, was 4.5.
